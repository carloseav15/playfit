Playfit

Not every acclaimed game is your game. Playfit helps you find the ones that are.

Problem

Players often choose by hype, then lose time forcing the wrong fit.

Popularity, review scores, and critical acclaim are useful for discovery. They are much less useful for telling a specific player what they are actually likely to enjoy and finish.

That gap creates a familiar pattern: start what feels culturally important, push through because it is “supposed” to be good, then stall, drop, or quietly move on.

What most discovery tools optimize for

  • Popularity
  • Review scores
  • Critical acclaim
  • Release visibility

What players actually need

  • What should I continue right now?
  • What should I start next?
  • What should I avoid even if it looks good?

What it does

A recommendation product built around personal fit and follow-through.

Prioritizes fit

It focuses on what is likely to work for a specific player, not on what is considered universally important.

Explains its suggestions

Recommendations should be understandable, not magical. The product highlights why something fits and why something may still be risky.

Respects real play behavior

It is designed to learn from liked games, dropped games, friction patterns, and platform access instead of treating every player the same.

Why it is different

It is not a backlog manager, a review site, or a popularity feed.

Not a backlog dump

The product is centered on better decisions, not on collecting endless lists of titles.

Not critic-first

A game can be acclaimed and still be the wrong fit for you. The system treats those as separate truths.

Not “one more score”

It aims to answer concrete decisions like continue, next up, resume, and avoid instead of hiding behind generic ratings.

Core outputs

The product is designed around a small set of useful decisions.

Current run

Keep the active game visible so momentum is not lost to recommendation churn.

Next up

Suggest the safest and strongest next candidate based on personal fit.

Avoid for now

Call out attractive-looking titles with a high chance of friction or abandonment.

Upcoming

Track future releases and surface which ones are worth paying attention to.
