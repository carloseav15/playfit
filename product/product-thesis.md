# Product Thesis

## Working Name

Taste Engine for Games

## One-Line Pitch

An AI-assisted game recommendation product that predicts what you are likely to enjoy and finish, while warning you away from titles that look appealing but are unlikely to fit your actual taste and play patterns.

## Problem

Most game discovery tools answer the wrong question.

They tell users:

- which games are popular
- which games are critically acclaimed
- which games are new

They do not answer:

- what this specific person is likely to enjoy
- what this person is likely to abandon
- what looks good in theory but repeatedly fails in practice

For players with clear taste patterns, large backlogs, and limited time, this creates a practical problem:

They spend time starting games that should have been filtered out earlier.

## Product Thesis

Game recommendation becomes much more valuable when the system is optimized for fit and follow-through, not generic quality.

This product should help a player answer four decisions:

1. What should I continue right now?
2. What should I start next?
3. What should I resume later?
4. What should I avoid even if it looks attractive?

## Core Insight

A good product in this space does not need to prove that a game is objectively great.

It needs to predict:

- personal affinity
- likelihood of abandonment
- whether the player is more likely to watch than play
- whether a game fits the player's current moment, platform access, and play behavior

## Why This Is Defensible

The project already combines three strong inputs:

- structured game metadata
- personal play history and opinion history
- explicit reasons for friction, pauses, and abandonment

That combination is more useful than generic review aggregation because it models player-specific taste rather than public consensus.

## Product Promise

The product promise should stay narrow:

It helps a player choose the next right game with explanations they can inspect.

Not included in the promise:

- universal catalog completeness
- social features
- critic review aggregation
- market-scale personalization
- perfect prediction

## Portfolio Value

As a portfolio piece, the project is strong because it shows:

- product framing, not just UI work
- structured data modeling
- explainable recommendation logic
- AI-assisted enrichment workflows
- clear tradeoffs between human truth and machine inference

## Product Boundaries

This should remain a small product in v1.

What to keep:

- current run
- next up
- best resume
- avoid for now
- upcoming releases with fit signals
- searchable affinity lookup

What to leave out of v1:

- multi-user accounts
- social graphs
- marketplace integrations
- “all games ever made” as a primary goal
- opaque machine learning that cannot explain recommendations

## Validation Thesis

The product is validated if it can demonstrate three things:

1. It can explain why a recommendation exists.
2. It can correctly identify some games that are appealing but risky for this user.
3. It can surface at least a few next-play recommendations that feel more accurate than generic gaming lists.
